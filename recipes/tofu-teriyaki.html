Tofu Teriyaki

  • 400 g extra firm tofu
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1/2 tsp fine kosher salt
  • black pepper
  • 1 ½ tablespoons vegetable or sesame oil
  • ⅓ cup finely chopped scallions, divided
  • 2 teaspoons minced or grated garlic
  • 1 ½ teaspoons fresh grated or minced ginger
  • ½ cup water
  • ⅓ cup low sodium soy sauce or tamari
  • 2 tablespoons mirin
  • 2 tablespoons rice wine vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 ½ tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 ½ teaspoon white sesame seeds
  • For serving: white rice, steamed broccoli or another vegetable of choice
  1. 1. Preheat oven to 190°C.
  2. 2. Drain the tofu well, pat dry and slice the block in half lengthwise, then wrap in a clean tea towel (or several paper towels). Set something heavy on top, like a cast iron skillet and press for 30 minutes.
  3. 3. Cube the tofu into 1 inch pieces. In a medium mixing bowl, toss the tofu gently in oil until well coated. Add in the cornstarch, salt, and pepper and toss until well coated.
  4. 4. Spread the tofu out on a parchment-lined baking sheet and roast for 25-30 minutes, stirring once or twice, until crispy and golden brown. 
  5. 5. Meanwhile, add oil to a large skillet over medium-low heat. Add ¼ cup scallions, garlic, and ginger and sauté for 5-6 minutes, stirring often to prevent the garlic from burning.
